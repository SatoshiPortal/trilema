Sunday, 12 October, Year 6 d.Tr. | Author: Mircea Popescu

Yeah, they're related.

First, as to the part of money and fucking, from the source of all thoughti :

Generally it's not understood that money is a burden, not a boon, and this current child porn cultural trendii is mixing the worst ingredients : child-hydrazine with money-methanol (it WILL make you blind, if you drink it!). So far all that's coming out of this recipe are wrecks.

I happen to be very fond of this particular expression. Now consider the context. For one thing, in Bitcoin is creating a whole new set of problems

This problem is currently - at least in my eyes - the largest issue plaguing Bitcoin. On one hand, its fascinating ease of use is possibly its greatest asset. On the other hand, kids’ ease in disregarding reality and highly esteeming themselves may not be their greatest asset, but it is statistically and from a species point of view their only utility. Neither can reasonably be made to go away, neither can sanely be dispensed of, and yet their contact creates a huge problem.

For the other, in The problem of too much money

Why ? Because there’s money, fake, bullshit, useless, fiat money constantly being pumped into the economy. This money is a fucking burden of the first degree on the shoulders of the people who do capital allocation, which is to say the investment bankers, fund managers and all the rest of the “evil” “financial elite”. You see, the curse of money is that you have to do something with it. You can’t simply take the Seinfeld out and “Why should my money work for me ? Let my money relax, I’ve worked hard enough for it.”

So what are you going to do with it ? Tin women, that’s what.

Child-hydrazine ? Check. Methanol money ? Check. Kaboom in three, two, one...

On the topic of money and lying, hmm... And at this juncture, allow me to dedicate the following explanation of the utility, importance and rational necessity of the bezzle and of the living wage to my engineering friends in #bitcoin-assets, who are certain to be blown away by this realisation, in loving memory of their much more comfortable uptake of my earlier explanations as to how corruption is equally important, useful and rationally necessary. That part was easy, because you're not invested in the bureaucracy. But to that set of snails, that idea is salt just like to you this idea is going to be unpleasant. Anyway, forward!

Money has two aspects, which are in fact unrelated. On one hand, money is the instrument of ensuring one's survival. You need money to pay the rent, and you need money to bail your idiot kid out of jail. This is all money, and any living being's natural tendency to acquire the means of life guarantees everyone will be forever trying to get money. How much of it ? A lot. How much is a lot ? Shut up and count, Bernie.

On the other hand, money is also the instrument of ensuring a group's survival, so to speak, through allocating resources back and forth. This is substantially different from that, much like cooking and eating are entirely different professions. You wouldn't trust a theatrical critic to write you a play, nor would you trust a playwright to get very far in the newspaper columns with his butthurt ideas, would you ? Same here!

This is what the bezzle is : an attempt to separate the first from the second. Because it makes sense to try and somehow separate them, because they're different things going different ways. Of course, there's also a lot of danger in trying to separate them, because for some abyssal reason nobody groks they don't seem to actually work apart. In that sense, an engine and a chassis are the components of all automobiles, be they cars planes trains submarines or anything else. Yet nobody to date has managed to build better automobiles by separating the chassis from the engine. Even if they are different things, and even if they are going different places.

Whereas the living wage is an attempt to lessen the pressure on the same set of problems going the other way : if nobody actually needs for anything, the theory goes, all sorts of people who aren't capital allocators but merely trying to ensure their own survival would stop getting in the way of things.

Neither of these works, of course, but the fact that they don't work doesn't actually make them or their proponents evil - they're trying something that actually makes sense. Of course, trying something that makes sense but doesn't work does make one wrong, and perseverence in error, especially if doubling down with fraud (pious or otherwise) and the array of sins therein attendant does make one evil. But that's an entirely different story.

To sum it all up, schematically : children have vast potential, which doesn't exist, as per the definition of potential. Society tends to defend itself from the dangers this potential poses through all sort of cognitive absurdities. Children seek money as the simple and obvious avenue to overturn whatever parts of those absurdities that's choking them to death. Society gives children money, because of said absurdities, and also pays a lot of very close attention to them (same reason). Children are thus "forced" to misrepresent themselves and their abilities. This pressure renders them insane and the whole thing blows up, only to be recollected by society, reinflated and... well... it blows up again.

In other words : economic cycles are in no sense economic. They're not actually social, either. They're the net result of specifically puritan idiocy, much like the gaunt, bloodless look of 1800s British girls. Their continuation in the "world" economy is simply a testament to the continued preeminence of puritans up top, which is why Japan could experience economic states that "aren't possible" for lengthy intervals, as a for instance.

The only possible solution to all this would be "decentralisation", after a fashion. Specifically :

  • Renounce the heretic belief that children are not ready. They're ready just as soon as they're ready, and if this outlook results in a few sad stories, fuck them.

  • Renounce the heretic belief that children are ready. They're ready just as soon as they're ready, and if this outlook results in a few sad stories, fuck them.

  • Renounce the heretic belief that acting out a life is just as good as living. Yeah, it's going to be painful. Fuck you.

For as long as the world continues to be dominated by children that magically pop into sexual maturity on their 18th birthday, the abuse of children as "Nobel prize laureates"iii will continue unabated. It's shameful, and sad, and doesn't actually serve anything. For as long as the world continues to be dominated by giving the appearance of success, the complete and utter rape of resources and ruin of productive factors will continue unabated. It's dubious we can long afford this. For as long as people continue insisting they can act with purpose and entertain a privileged relation with the future, instead of sanely proceeding from causes and letting the future be nobody will be able to understand anything, and what's worse, everyone will be able to claim they understood everything, and you'll never know the difference. It's a sickening display, honestly, and getting quite tiresome in its flat recursivity.

Time for a little actual change maybe ?
